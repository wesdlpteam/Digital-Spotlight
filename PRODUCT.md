# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Wesley College teachers, across IB PYP (Prep–Y6), MYP (Y7–10), and Senior (Y11–12) bands. They use this **between classes or while planning** — short on time, often not technical, and preparing student-facing content. The job to be done: turn a piece of stimulus (an image, an article/PDF, or a video transcript) into a ready-to-run **5-minute Digital Space Spotlight** discussion — and walk away with an editable, Wesley-branded PowerPoint they can drop straight into a lesson.

## Product Purpose

A single-page tool that generates cyber-safety and digital-citizenship discussion prompts aligned to the right IB band, scaffolds them with a rotating Visible Thinking routine, and exports a real, editable `.pptx` in the Wesley College theme. It exists to remove the prep friction from running regular, timely conversations about students' digital lives — making a good teaching habit fast enough to actually keep. Success = a teacher arrives with a stimulus and leaves, in a couple of minutes, with a slide deck they trust and can run as-is.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, school-professional. Three words: **calm · trustworthy · clear.** The tool handles student-facing and AI-generated content, so the interface should feel safe and unflashy — it reassures rather than excites. Quiet confidence over cleverness. Wesley's purple-and-gold identity is present as accent and in the export, but the working UI stays restrained so the teacher's content is the focus, not the chrome.

## Anti-references

- **Consumer-AI flash** — no glowing gradients, sparkle/magic-wand motifs, or "powered by AI" theatrics. The AI is plumbing, not the point.
- **Generic SaaS dashboard** — no metric-card grids, no analytics-tool density. This is a focused single-task tool, not a console.
- **Gimmicky edtech** — no cartoon mascots, no over-rounded "playful" everything, no badge/gamification noise. Teachers want a professional instrument.
- **Cramped utility form** — equally, not a bare grey settings form. Restrained is not the same as cold.

## Design Principles

- **The teacher's content is the hero.** Chrome recedes; the generated slides and editable fields get the visual weight and the clearest contrast.
- **Two-minute path.** Every screen should move a hurried teacher closer to a finished deck. No step exists that doesn't earn its place in that path.
- **Trustworthy by default.** Because output is AI-generated and student-facing, the UI signals safety, editability, and control (clear edit affordances, a visible Forget button for the API key, honest loading/error states).
- **Calm restraint.** Soft violet surfaces, one accent at a time, generous breathing room. Energy comes from typography and spacing, not saturation.
- **On-brand without shouting.** Wesley purple/gold reads as quiet identity in the app and full brand in the exported `.pptx` — never as decorative noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** — body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, visible focus states, labelled inputs and controls, honest status messaging.
- **Keyboard-first** — full keyboard operability assumed (teachers on laptops, no mouse): logical tab order, focus-visible everywhere, escape/enter on modals, no keyboard traps in the editable preview fields.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any transition or animation as it is added.
